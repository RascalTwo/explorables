FROM (MGM+, 2022) is a sci-fi horror mystery about an ordinary American town that traps everyone who drives into it. Every road out bends back to where you started. And when the sun goes down, things that look like people come out of the forest — and they want in.

Genre · Sci-fi horror mystery Created by John Griffin Exec. prod. Jack Bender (Lost) Season 1 · 10 episodes

01Rules of the Town

Nobody chose to be here. The residents have learned the hard way what keeps you breathing. Newcomers learn them in their first terrifying night — or they don't.

🛣️

You can't drive out

Every road loops. Drive straight for hours and the town reappears ahead of you. The forest won't let go.

🌙

Be inside by dark

The creatures only come out at night. Survival is a daily race to get behind a door before sundown.

🪬

Wear a talisman

Stone-and-glass talismans hung over a doorway stop the creatures from entering. While it hangs, the house is safe.

🚪

Never invite them in

They smile. They sound human. They beg to be let inside. The moment you open the door for them, the protection means nothing.

The town is small — a main street, a scatter of homes, and the woods pressing in on every side. These are the places Season 1 keeps returning to.

03Two Communities, One Trap

The trapped split into two camps with very different philosophies about how to live inside a nightmare.

The Township

Led by Sheriff Boyd Stevens · the diner, the church, the clinic
  • Rules, order, and grim routine. Boyd holds the community together by force of will.
  • Believes survival means discipline: count heads, ration hope, keep digging for a way out.
  • Home base for the newly-arrived Matthews family and most long-time residents.
vs

Colony House

Led by Donna · a big communal house at the edge of town
  • A looser, commune-style household — share everything, party against the dread, look after your own.
  • Skeptical of Boyd's authority; thinks the answer is acceptance, not control.
  • Where some younger residents (including Boyd's own son) choose to live.

05The Things at Night

The show is careful never to fully explain them. Here's only what the residents have observed.

  • They look like us. Men, women, even children — often in old-fashioned clothes, always with a warm, wrong smile.
  • They're nocturnal. Daylight is safe; they emerge from the treeline at dusk and vanish by dawn.
  • They manipulate. They mimic loved ones, plead, and reason — anything to get you to open a door.
  • They're patient and brutal. Once inside, the violence is sudden and total. No bargaining works.
  • Talismans hold them off. The only reliable defense — though no one in town truly knows why they work.
