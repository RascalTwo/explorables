The Euro Step

Basketball's slickest finishing move — dodging a defender at the rim, legally. Five ways to see it.
Ball-handler Defender Ball

The idea: instead of jumping straight at the rim into a waiting defender, you take your two allowed steps in two different directions — one way to make the defender commit, then the other way to slip past for the layup. Hit ▶ and watch the green dot.

Why it's legal: once you stop dribbling (the “gather”), the rules give you two steps. The Euro Step just uses those two steps to zig-zag instead of going straight.

The trick isn't speed — it's commitment

A defender must honor your first step, so they lean to cut it off. The instant they do, their body has momentum that way (red arrow). Humans can't reverse momentum instantly — for a few tenths of a second they're carried the way they committed.

The second step attacks the space they vacated

Your second step goes the opposite way. The reachable circle shifts toward the fake — that shift is the whole move. Drag D around: it's hardest to beat a defender who sits back and doesn't commit, which is exactly why a good Euro Step sells the fake to force the lean.

The same move, in 3D

Now you can see what the top-down view flattens: the lean. On step 1 the body tilts toward the fake to sell it; on step 2 it whips the other way while the ball swings across the body, shielded from the defender.

The two long, low strides — far wider than a normal running step — are what cover the ground sideways. That's the Euro Step's signature look: not faster, just wider and in two directions.

The gather is “step zero” — picking up your dribble. Then you get exactly two steps.

Why two steps, in two directions

The whole move lives inside one rule: the moment you stop dribbling — the gather — you may take two steps before you shoot or pass. Most layups spend those two steps going straight. The Euro Step spends them going two different ways: one to move the defender, one to beat them.

Take a third step (or move your pivot foot before the gather) and it's a travel — a turnover. Watch the counter: step 3 is where it turns red. Selling a huge fake while staying inside two steps is the skill.
